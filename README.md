## 🌟 Highlights

* **The Closed-Learning Loop:** Automatically shadow-routes traffic to intercept undocumented business rules during system migrations.
* **Self-Correcting Architecture:** Catches missing edge-cases (e.g., dynamic currency conversions) and logs them immutably before production crashes.
* **Trillion-Dollar Market Fit:** Solves "The Institutional Knowledge Void" that causes 70% of enterprise legacy migrations to fail.

## ℹ️ Overview 
When enterprise companies attempt to modernize legacy systems, they don't fail because of bad code; they fail because decades of unwritten business rules and edge-case patches are lost the moment the old servers are turned off. 

**The Archivist Protocol** is a self-correcting validation layer between old and new systems. Instead of forcing engineers to guess the legacy rules, the protocol discovers them autonomously. By duplicating live traffic to both systems and algorithmically comparing the outputs, it catches discrepancies and logs the expected behavior into a secure database—capturing lost institutional knowledge in real-time.

## 🛠️ Tech Stack & Frameworks
This entire architecture was synthesized autonomously by the Hermes agent acting as a high-agency meta-tool.

* **Agent Engine:** Hermes-4-405B (Nous Research Inference API)
* **Backend:** Node.js / Express.js
* **Database:** Neon Serverless PostgreSQL (`legacy_rules` and `shadow_traffic_logs`)
* **Deployment:** Vercel Edge Network
* **Frontend UI:** Vanilla JS + Tailwind CSS (Cursor IDE Design System)
